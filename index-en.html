The Firm

Background in Public and Corporate Law since 1997

Based in Brasília, the firm focuses on complex legal matters that require careful analysis, institutional awareness and strategic treatment.

Established in 1997, the firm has developed a practice focused on solving issues involving Administrative Law, state-owned companies, public contracts, strategic civil litigation, Corporate Law, M&A and governance.

The work combines technical rigor, a systemic reading of the legal framework and constant dialogue with case law, especially from the Superior Courts and Audit Courts, without losing sight of the clients’ business and institutional reality.

The firm adopts a calm, discreet and objective stance, with clear and realistic communication about risks, alternatives and available legal paths, always in strict compliance with Bar Association standards.

Texts and reflections by the firm

Below are three short pieces that illustrate how the firm approaches complex legal matters.

Article
State-owned companies, governance and accountability

State-owned companies occupy a unique position: they are expected to deliver results in competitive environments while remaining subject to public law controls. This hybrid regime demands decisions that reconcile business strategy, regulatory compliance and integrity rules.

Sound governance depends on internal controls, risk management, transparency and a clear allocation of responsibilities among managers, boards and technical areas. When these elements are weak, the usual outcome is an increase in disputes, sanctions by oversight bodies and loss of economic and reputational value.

Informative text only, not a substitute for case-specific legal advice or formal opinions.

Article
When is litigation worthwhile? Objective criteria

Filing a lawsuit or an administrative challenge should not be an automatic reflex, but a strategic choice. Litigation consumes time, institutional energy and financial resources, and may expose companies or public entities to adverse precedents and increased liabilities.

A sound decision is guided by objective criteria: the quality of available evidence; consistency of the legal theory with prevailing case law; financial and reputational impact of the dispute; feasibility of a negotiated or administrative solution; and indirect costs involved. Not everything that is legally possible is, at the same time, institutionally desirable.

Informative text only, not intended as individualized legal advice.

Article
Technical advocacy without spectacle: limits of legal communication

Modern legal practice requires a digital presence, but this does not authorize turning the profession into a show. Law firm and lawyer communication should remain informative, measured and compatible with the limits set by Bar Association rules on advertising and public information.

This means avoiding promises of specific outcomes, unfair comparisons, sensational exposure of cases and language that might mislead the public. The focus should be on clear explanations of relevant legal topics, responsible presentation of professional experience and transparency about the limitations of law as a conflict-resolution tool.

Informative text only, in line with ethical and disciplinary rules governing the legal profession.
